CARL ATOGO

Carl Atogo, born in Lagos, Nigeria, in 1980, is a respected civil rights attorney and activist. He gained national recognition for his role in advocating for human rights and justice reforms across Africa. After earning his law degree from Oxford University, Carl returned home to fight against systemic injustices, representing marginalized communities in high-profile cases. His work led to the passing of several progressive laws aimed at protecting minority rights. Carl is also the founder of the Atogo Legal Aid Foundation, which provides pro bono legal assistance to those in need. His unwavering dedication has earned him multiple international awards, making him a symbol of justice and equality.

MASHA ATOGO

Masha Atogo, born in Accra, Ghana, in 1992, is an influential environmental scientist, activist, and author known for her groundbreaking work in climate change resilience and sustainable agriculture. Growing up in a rural community, she witnessed firsthand the devastating effects of deforestation and soil degradation, sparking her passion for environmental conservation. She pursued a degree in Environmental Science at Harvard University and later completed a Ph.D. in Climate Policy at Oxford. Masha founded Green Earth Initiative, a non-profit organization dedicated to restoring ecosystems and promoting sustainable farming practices. She has worked with global organizations like the United Nations and has been featured in National Geographic for her pioneering research. In addition to her activism, Masha has authored two best-selling books on environmental preservation. She continues to inspire the world through her work in policy-making and hands-on conservation efforts.
